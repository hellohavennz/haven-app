# 2.3 – Britain Since 1945 (Verified Reference)

## 🇬🇧 Verified Key Facts

- The period after **1945** was one of reconstruction, reform, and transformation for the United Kingdom.  
- Following the end of the **Second World War**, Britain faced significant economic damage and social change.  
- The **Labour government (1945–1951)**, led by **Clement Attlee**, introduced major welfare reforms and established the **National Health Service (NHS)** in **1948**, providing healthcare free at the point of use.  
- The government also created a comprehensive **social security system**, expanded education, and nationalised key industries such as coal, steel, and railways.  
- Britain’s role as a global imperial power declined as many colonies gained independence in the process of **decolonisation** during the 1950s and 1960s.  
- The transformation of the **British Empire** into the **Commonwealth of Nations** encouraged cooperation among newly independent countries.  
- The **1950s and 1960s** were characterised by economic growth, technological innovation, and the rise of youth culture.  
- The **“Swinging Sixties”** saw major social changes in music, fashion, and attitudes, symbolised by London’s cultural influence.  
- The **1970s** brought economic difficulties, including inflation, industrial strikes, and energy crises.  
- In **1973**, Britain joined the **European Economic Community (EEC)**, later the **European Union (EU)**, strengthening trade ties with Europe.  
- **Margaret Thatcher**, Prime Minister from **1979 to 1990**, introduced policies of economic liberalisation — privatising state-owned industries, limiting trade union power, and reducing government spending.  
- Her government emphasised free-market economics and individual responsibility, though these policies also led to social divisions and unemployment in some regions.  
- The **1990s** saw a shift under **Tony Blair’s Labour government (1997–2007)**, which introduced **devolution** — creating the **Scottish Parliament**, **Welsh Assembly**, and **Northern Ireland Assembly**.  
- The **Good Friday Agreement (1998)** helped bring peace to Northern Ireland after decades of conflict known as “The Troubles.”  
- The early **21st century** saw Britain engaged in international military operations, including in **Afghanistan (2001)** and **Iraq (2003)**, in partnership with the United States.  
- The **2008 global financial crisis** affected the UK economy, leading to austerity measures and cuts to public services.  
- In **2016**, the British public voted to **leave the European Union (Brexit)**, a process completed in **2020**.  
- Britain has continued to face challenges relating to economic recovery, social cohesion, and maintaining unity among its four nations.  
- The UK remains a **constitutional monarchy** and **parliamentary democracy**, with a continued commitment to fairness, equality, and the rule of law.

---

## 💡 Concept Summary (for Memory Hook)

Post-war Britain can be viewed as a **cycle of rebuilding and renewal** — from reconstruction after 1945 to modern challenges of globalisation and national identity. It reflects the UK’s ability to adapt, reform, and lead through change while maintaining its democratic and social foundations.
