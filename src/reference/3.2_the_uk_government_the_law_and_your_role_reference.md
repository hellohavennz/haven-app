# 3.2 – The UK Government, the Law, and Your Role (Verified Reference)

## 🇬🇧 Verified Key Facts

- The United Kingdom is governed through a **constitutional framework** that separates powers among the **executive**, **legislative**, and **judicial** branches.  
- The **executive** (the government) implements laws and runs public services, led by the **Prime Minister** and **Cabinet ministers**.  
- The **legislature** (Parliament) makes and debates laws through the **House of Commons** and the **House of Lords**.  
- The **judiciary** (courts) interprets and enforces the law, remaining independent from government influence.  
- The UK’s legal system is based on the **rule of law**, meaning everyone — including the government — is subject to the law.  
- There are two main types of law in the UK:  
  - **Criminal law**, which deals with offences against society.  
  - **Civil law**, which handles disputes between individuals or organisations.  
- The **police** are responsible for maintaining public order, preventing and investigating crime, and protecting life and property.  
- Police officers must act within the law and are accountable to the public and the courts.  
- The **Crown Prosecution Service (CPS)** brings criminal cases to court on behalf of the state in England and Wales.  
- The **courts** administer justice fairly, ensuring the right to a fair trial and the presumption of innocence until proven guilty.  
- In criminal cases, a **jury** of 12 citizens is used in serious trials to decide guilt or innocence based on the evidence presented.  
- The **Magistrates’ Courts** handle minor offences, while more serious cases go to the **Crown Court**.  
- The **Supreme Court of the United Kingdom**, established in **2009**, is the highest court and handles cases of constitutional or public importance.  
- **Solicitors** and **barristers** represent clients and ensure that justice is properly served within the court system.  
- The **Human Rights Act 1998** incorporated the **European Convention on Human Rights** into UK law, protecting rights such as freedom of speech, religion, and fair trial.  
- Every person living in the UK has legal rights and responsibilities.  
- Citizens and residents are expected to:  
  - **Obey the law** and respect the rights of others.  
  - **Serve on a jury** if called.  
  - **Pay taxes** and contribute to society.  
  - **Participate in democratic processes** when eligible to vote.  
- The **media** also plays an important role in ensuring accountability by reporting on political and legal matters.  
- Individuals can contribute to justice and fairness by supporting community safety, respecting the law, and staying informed about their rights.

## 💡 Concept Summary (for Memory Hook)

The relationship between the law, government, and the citizen can be pictured as a **scales of justice** — one side represents the power of the state to enforce the law, and the other represents the rights and responsibilities of the individual. Balance between these ensures freedom, fairness, and stability in society.
