# 3.3 – The UK and International Institutions (Verified Reference)

## 🇬🇧 Verified Key Facts

- The United Kingdom plays an active role in international organisations that promote peace, security, human rights, and economic cooperation.  
- The **United Nations (UN)** was established in **1945** after the Second World War to maintain international peace and security.  
- The UK is one of the **five permanent members** of the **UN Security Council**, alongside the United States, France, Russia, and China.  
- As a permanent member, the UK has the right to **veto** Security Council resolutions and contributes to decisions on global conflict resolution and humanitarian action.  
- The **North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO)** was founded in **1949** as a military alliance to ensure the collective defence of its member countries.  
- NATO’s founding principle, under **Article 5**, states that an attack on one member is considered an attack on all.  
- The UK is a founding member of NATO and contributes armed forces, resources, and leadership to its missions.  
- The **Commonwealth** is a voluntary association of over **50 independent nations**, many of which were formerly part of the British Empire.  
- The **Head of the Commonwealth** is the **British monarch**, symbolising shared history and values among member countries.  
- The **Commonwealth Charter** promotes democracy, human rights, gender equality, and sustainable development.  
- The **Council of Europe**, established in **1949**, aims to uphold human rights, democracy, and the rule of law across Europe.  
- The **European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR)**, created by the Council of Europe, sets out fundamental rights and freedoms; it is enforced by the **European Court of Human Rights** in Strasbourg.  
- The **Human Rights Act 1998** incorporated the ECHR into UK domestic law, allowing individuals to seek justice in UK courts for human rights violations.  
- The **World Trade Organization (WTO)** oversees international trade rules, aiming to ensure free and fair global trade; the UK is an active member.  
- The **Group of Seven (G7)** is an organisation of major advanced economies — the UK, USA, Canada, France, Germany, Italy, and Japan — that meets to discuss global economic policy.  
- The **North Atlantic Council**, part of NATO, and the **UN General Assembly** are key forums where the UK engages diplomatically.  
- The **Organisation for Security and Co-operation in Europe (OSCE)** promotes peace, stability, and democracy in Europe, and the UK is a participating state.  
- The UK contributes to international peacekeeping, humanitarian aid, and development programmes through these organisations.  
- Participation in these international institutions strengthens the UK’s global influence and commitment to cooperative solutions for global challenges such as climate change, security, and human rights.

## 💡 Concept Summary (for Memory Hook)

The UK’s relationship with international institutions can be viewed as a **web of cooperation**, where each strand — the UN, NATO, the Commonwealth, and others — connects the nation to shared goals of peace, democracy, and development. These links form a network that supports global stability and reflects the UK’s ongoing international leadership.
