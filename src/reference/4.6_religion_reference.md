# 4.6 – Religion (Verified Reference)

## 🇬🇧 Verified Key Facts

- The United Kingdom has a long history of religious belief, tolerance, and diversity, with Christianity remaining the largest religion.  
- The UK is a **secular democracy**, meaning there is freedom of religion and belief, and individuals are free to practise any faith or none.  
- The **Church of England** is the **established church** in England. It is both Protestant and Catholic in tradition and structure.  
- The **Monarch** is the **Supreme Governor** of the Church of England and appoints senior bishops on the advice of the Prime Minister.  
- The most senior cleric in the Church of England is the **Archbishop of Canterbury**, followed by the **Archbishop of York**.  
- The Church of England plays a role in public life; **26 bishops** sit in the **House of Lords** as the **Lords Spiritual**.  
- The **Church of Scotland** (the **Kirk**) is **Presbyterian** in structure and independent of the state.  
- **Wales** and **Northern Ireland** do not have established churches.  
- Other Christian denominations in the UK include **Roman Catholic**, **Baptist**, **Methodist**, and **Pentecostal** churches.  
- The **Christian calendar** shapes many public holidays, including **Christmas** and **Easter**.  
- The UK also recognises and respects the beliefs of other major world religions, including:  
  - **Islam**, with followers known as **Muslims**, who worship in **mosques**.  
  - **Hinduism**, with worship in **temples** dedicated to deities such as Vishnu and Shiva.  
  - **Sikhism**, with followers worshipping in **Gurdwaras**.  
  - **Judaism**, with **synagogues** as places of worship and **Sabbath** observance from Friday evening to Saturday evening.  
  - **Buddhism**, focusing on meditation and the teachings of the **Buddha**.  
- The 2021 Census recorded increasing religious diversity and a growing number of people identifying as having **no religion**.  
- The right to **freedom of thought, conscience, and religion** is protected under the **Human Rights Act 1998**.  
- Schools in the UK are legally required to teach about different religions to promote understanding and respect.  
- Interfaith organisations, such as the **Inter Faith Network for the UK**, work to promote dialogue and cooperation between communities of different faiths.  
- Religious buildings, from cathedrals and mosques to synagogues and temples, form part of the UK’s cultural and architectural heritage.  
- Religion continues to influence moral values, social care, and charity work across the country.  
- The UK’s approach to religion is guided by respect, inclusivity, and the belief that diverse faiths can coexist peacefully within one society.

## 💡 Concept Summary (for Memory Hook)

Religion in the UK can be seen as a **mosaic of belief** — many different faiths and traditions forming one picture of a society that values freedom, tolerance, and understanding. Each piece contributes to the nation’s shared cultural and moral framework.
