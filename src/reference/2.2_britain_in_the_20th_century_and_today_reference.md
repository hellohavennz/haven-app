# 2.2 – Britain in the 20th Century and Today (Verified Reference)

## 🇬🇧 Verified Key Facts

- The **20th century** was a period of rapid change for Britain in politics, society, and technology.  
- The **First World War (1914–1918)** marked a turning point, leading to massive loss of life and major social transformation.  
- After the war, women gained greater independence, and the **Representation of the People Acts (1918 and 1928)** extended voting rights to women on equal terms with men.  
- The **Second World War (1939–1945)** reshaped Britain once again; under **Winston Churchill’s** leadership, the nation resisted invasion and fought for democracy.  
- Post-war recovery included the creation of the **welfare state** and the establishment of the **National Health Service (NHS)** in **1948**, providing free healthcare at the point of use.  
- The government also introduced **social housing**, **unemployment benefits**, and **pensions** to improve living standards.  
- Britain underwent major **decolonisation** after 1945, granting independence to many former colonies and transforming the **British Empire** into the **Commonwealth of Nations**.  
- The **1950s and 1960s** were years of social mobility, economic growth, and cultural transformation.  
- Britain became known for its influence in **music**, **fashion**, and **popular culture**, including figures like **The Beatles**, **The Rolling Stones**, and designers such as **Mary Quant**.  
- The **1970s** were marked by economic difficulties, strikes, and political challenges, including debates over Britain’s membership in the **European Economic Community (EEC)**, which it joined in **1973**.  
- **Margaret Thatcher**, the UK’s first female Prime Minister (1979–1990), introduced major economic reforms, promoting privatisation, reducing union power, and encouraging individual enterprise.  
- The **Labour government under Tony Blair (1997–2007)** introduced **devolution**, giving new powers to **Scotland**, **Wales**, and **Northern Ireland**.  
- The **Good Friday Agreement (1998)** brought peace to Northern Ireland after decades of conflict known as “The Troubles.”  
- The **21st century** has seen continuing political, social, and technological change, including the **global financial crisis (2008)** and debates about immigration, identity, and Britain’s role in the world.  
- In **2016**, a national referendum decided that the UK would **leave the European Union (Brexit)**, which formally occurred in **2020**.  
- The UK remains a **constitutional monarchy** and **democracy**, with a commitment to individual rights, equality before the law, and active participation in international organisations.  
- Modern Britain faces ongoing challenges, including economic inequality, climate change, and maintaining national unity across its four nations.  
- Despite challenges, the UK continues to be a leading global power in science, culture, finance, and innovation.

## 💡 Concept Summary (for Memory Hook)

Britain in the 20th century can be pictured as a **journey from empire to equality** — a story of rebuilding, reform, and renewal. From war and recovery to welfare and modern democracy, the UK evolved into a society focused on fairness, opportunity, and resilience.
