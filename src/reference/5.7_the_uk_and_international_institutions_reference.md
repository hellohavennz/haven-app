# 5.7 – The UK and International Institutions (Verified Reference)

## 🇬🇧 Verified Key Facts

- The United Kingdom plays an active role in international institutions that promote peace, democracy, human rights, and economic development.  
- International cooperation is a cornerstone of modern British foreign policy.  

### The United Nations (UN)
- The **United Nations (UN)** was founded in **1945** to maintain peace and security and to promote cooperation between nations.  
- The UK is a **founding member** of the UN and one of the **five permanent members** of the **UN Security Council**, alongside the United States, France, Russia, and China.  
- As a permanent member, the UK holds the **power of veto** on Security Council resolutions.  
- The UK contributes to UN **peacekeeping missions**, **humanitarian programmes**, and global **sustainable development goals**.  
- British diplomats and peacekeepers serve in many UN operations around the world.

### The Commonwealth
- The **Commonwealth of Nations**, often called simply the **Commonwealth**, is a voluntary association of more than **50 independent countries**, many of which were formerly part of the British Empire.  
- The **British Monarch** serves as the **Head of the Commonwealth**, symbolising unity among member nations.  
- Member countries cooperate to promote **democracy**, **development**, **human rights**, and **education**.  
- The **Commonwealth Charter (2013)** sets out shared values, including gender equality and the rule of law.  
- Events such as the **Commonwealth Games** foster friendship and cultural exchange among members.

### The North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO)
- **NATO** is a **military alliance** founded in **1949** to ensure the collective defence of its member states.  
- Its guiding principle, **Article 5**, states that an attack on one member is considered an attack on all.  
- The UK is a **founding member** of NATO and contributes armed forces, intelligence, and strategic support to its operations.  
- NATO’s purpose has evolved to include counterterrorism, cyber defence, and humanitarian missions.

### The Council of Europe
- The **Council of Europe**, established in **1949**, is separate from the European Union and promotes **human rights**, **democracy**, and the **rule of law**.  
- The UK is a founding member and helped draft the **European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR)**.  
- The **European Court of Human Rights**, based in **Strasbourg**, interprets and enforces the Convention.  
- The **Human Rights Act 1998** incorporated the ECHR into UK law, allowing individuals to bring human rights cases before UK courts.

### The Group of Seven (G7)
- The **G7** is an informal group of leading advanced economies: the **UK, USA, Canada, France, Germany, Italy, and Japan**.  
- It meets annually to discuss global economic stability, security, and climate issues.  
- The UK often plays a leading role in shaping G7 policies on trade, sustainability, and international aid.

### The World Trade Organization (WTO)
- The **WTO** regulates global trade and ensures that trade flows smoothly, predictably, and freely.  
- The UK became an independent member of the WTO after leaving the European Union in **2020**.  
- It supports free trade and works to resolve trade disputes between nations.

### The Organisation for Security and Co-operation in Europe (OSCE)
- The **OSCE** promotes peace, stability, and democracy across Europe and neighbouring regions.  
- The UK participates in election monitoring and supports peacebuilding efforts through this organisation.

- Through membership in these institutions, the UK promotes global stability, upholds human rights, and contributes to international law and security.  
- Active engagement in global affairs reflects Britain’s long-standing commitment to diplomacy, cooperation, and shared progress.
