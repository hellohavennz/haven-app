# 1.4 – A Modern, Thriving Society (Verified Reference)

## 🇬🇧 Verified Key Facts

- The United Kingdom is a diverse, developed, and modern society that combines long-standing traditions with social and technological progress.  
- The UK has a population of approximately **67 million people**, with communities from a wide range of cultural, ethnic, and religious backgrounds.  
- English is the main language spoken, but many other languages are used, reflecting the UK’s multicultural population.  
- The country is composed of **England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland**, each with its own identity and traditions.  
- The UK is a **constitutional monarchy** and a **parliamentary democracy**, meaning the monarch remains Head of State while elected representatives govern the country.  
- The **Union Flag (Union Jack)** represents the unity of the four nations and is the national flag of the United Kingdom.  
- The **National Anthem**, *“God Save the King”*, is performed at official and public events.  
- British society values **freedom of speech, tolerance, equality, and the rule of law**.  
- Citizens and residents are protected by rights such as freedom of religion, expression, and association.  
- The UK has an established welfare system providing financial and practical support to individuals in need, including pensions, healthcare, and disability assistance.  
- The **National Health Service (NHS)**, founded in **1948**, provides free medical care at the point of use and is funded through taxation.  
- The UK education system is compulsory for children between the ages of **5 and 18**, with most pupils attending publicly funded schools.  
- Higher education, including universities and vocational colleges, is internationally respected, with institutions such as **Oxford**, **Cambridge**, and **Edinburgh** known worldwide.  
- The UK economy is one of the largest in the world, based on services, finance, manufacturing, and technology.  
- London is a global financial centre, while other major cities like **Birmingham**, **Manchester**, **Glasgow**, and **Cardiff** contribute significantly to commerce and innovation.  
- The UK is home to a wide variety of cultural institutions, including museums, theatres, and galleries, which reflect the country’s artistic heritage and modern creativity.  
- British media, such as the **BBC**, plays a major role in informing and educating the public.  
- The UK continues to adapt to changes in global technology, communication, and sustainability, maintaining its influence in world culture, science, and innovation.

## 💡 Concept Summary (for Memory Hook)

Modern Britain can be pictured as a **living mosaic** — a pattern made up of many pieces representing its people, institutions, and traditions. Each piece, whether cultural, economic, or political, contributes to a society that balances **heritage with progress** and **diversity with shared national values**.
