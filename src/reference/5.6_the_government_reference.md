# 5.6 – The Government (Verified Reference)

## 🇬🇧 Verified Key Facts

- The **Government of the United Kingdom** is responsible for running the country, creating policy, and ensuring that laws passed by Parliament are implemented.  
- The UK has a **constitutional monarchy** and a **parliamentary democracy**, meaning the Government is accountable to Parliament and, through it, to the people.  
- The **Prime Minister (PM)** is the Head of Government and leads the **executive branch**.  
- The PM is appointed by the **Monarch**, usually as the leader of the political party with a majority in the **House of Commons**.  
- The Prime Minister:  
  - Chairs meetings of the **Cabinet**.  
  - Represents the UK domestically and internationally.  
  - Oversees government policy and national security.  
  - Advises the Monarch on major appointments and the use of royal powers.  
- The **Cabinet** consists of about **20 senior ministers**, each responsible for a government department (e.g., Foreign Affairs, Defence, Health, Education).  
- Cabinet members are collectively responsible for government decisions — this is known as **collective responsibility**.  
- The **Chancellor of the Exchequer** manages the nation’s finances, taxation, and economic policy.  
- The **Home Secretary** oversees internal affairs, including policing, immigration, and national security.  
- The **Foreign Secretary** manages foreign relations and represents the UK abroad.  
- Other ministers head departments such as the **Department for Education**, **Department of Health and Social Care**, and **Ministry of Defence**.  
- Below the Cabinet are **junior ministers** and **parliamentary under-secretaries** who assist with departmental duties.  
- The **Civil Service** supports the government by providing expert, politically neutral advice and ensuring that policies are implemented efficiently.  
- Civil servants are required to act with integrity, impartiality, and honesty.  
- The **Opposition**, led by the **Leader of the Opposition**, provides alternative policies and holds the Government to account through debate and scrutiny.  
- Parliament scrutinises government decisions through **select committees**, **question periods**, and **debates**.  
- The **Prime Minister’s Questions (PMQs)**, held weekly, allows MPs to question the Prime Minister directly.  
- The **Monarch’s Speech** at the **State Opening of Parliament** outlines the Government’s legislative agenda for the coming year.  
- **Devolved governments** in Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland are responsible for certain areas such as education, health, and transport within their nations.  
- **Local government** delivers local services including housing, planning, and waste management.  
- The UK Government represents the country in international organisations such as the **United Nations (UN)**, **NATO**, and the **Commonwealth of Nations**.  
- The Government must retain the **confidence of the House of Commons** to remain in office.  
- If it loses a **vote of confidence**, a **general election** may be called.  
- Effective government depends on transparency, accountability, and respect for democratic processes.  

---

## 💡 Concept Summary (for Memory Hook)

The UK Government can be pictured as a **wheel of responsibility** — the Prime Minister and Cabinet steer the nation, Parliament ensures accountability, and the Civil Service keeps the wheel turning through expertise and impartial action.
