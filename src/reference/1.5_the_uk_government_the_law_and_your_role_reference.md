# 1.5 – The UK Government, the Law, and Your Role (Verified Reference)

## 🇬🇧 Verified Key Facts

- The United Kingdom is a **parliamentary democracy** with a **constitutional monarch**.  
- The **monarch** is the **Head of State**, while the **Prime Minister** is the **Head of Government**.  
- The UK Parliament, located at **Westminster in London**, is the main legislative body responsible for making and passing laws.  
- Parliament consists of **two Houses**:  
  - The **House of Commons**, whose Members of Parliament (MPs) are elected by the public.  
  - The **House of Lords**, made up of life peers, bishops, and hereditary peers.  
- Laws are proposed in Parliament as **Bills** and must be approved by both Houses and the monarch before becoming **Acts of Parliament**.  
- The **Prime Minister** leads the government, selects ministers, and heads the **Cabinet**, which makes key policy decisions.  
- The **Civil Service** supports government ministers in implementing policies and delivering public services.  
- The **judiciary** is independent from government and Parliament, ensuring fairness and the rule of law.  
- The **Supreme Court** is the highest court in the United Kingdom and deals with complex or constitutional legal issues.  
- The **police** maintain law and order, prevent crime, and protect citizens, operating under the principle of public consent.  
- The **Crown Prosecution Service (CPS)** prosecutes criminal cases in England and Wales on behalf of the state.  
- The UK has **devolved governments** in **Scotland**, **Wales**, and **Northern Ireland**, each with its own parliament or assembly and varying degrees of legislative power.  
- **Local government** manages community-level services such as education, transport, planning, housing, and waste collection.  
- **Elections** in the UK are conducted through a **secret ballot** to ensure fairness and freedom from coercion.  
- Citizens aged **18 or over** who are registered to vote may participate in general, local, and devolved elections, as well as referendums.  
- The UK’s legal system is based on the principle that everyone is **innocent until proven guilty** and that **justice must be applied equally** to all.  
- Individuals have a civic duty to **obey the law**, **serve on a jury** when called, and **respect the decisions of courts**.  
- People can influence decision-making by voting, contacting their MP, joining political parties, or participating in lawful protests.  
- British citizenship carries with it not only rights and freedoms but also responsibilities to uphold the country’s laws and democratic principles.

## 💡 Concept Summary (for Memory Hook)

The structure of government in the UK can be imagined as a **balanced triangle**: Parliament makes the laws, the government enforces them, and the courts interpret them. Each side supports the others, ensuring fairness, accountability, and the protection of citizens’ rights within a democratic framework.
